How to Write an Effective Report

Be specific and clear: Include details about what's broken, when it happened, and how it affects the space.

Best Practices:

  • Include specific room numbers and equipment identifiers
  • Describe the problem, not just symptoms
  • Mention safety concerns if applicable
  • Note if the issue affects accessibility
  • Include time-sensitive information

Examples:

Good: "The main entrance door handle is completely broken off. Students cannot open the door from outside, creating a safety issue during emergency evacuations."
Good: "Women's restroom on 2nd floor is completely out of toilet paper in all 4 stalls."
Poor: "Door doesn't work" - Doesn't describe what door or why the door doesn't work
